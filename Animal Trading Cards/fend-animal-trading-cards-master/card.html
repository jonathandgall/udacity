Violet-backed starling

It is rarely seen on the ground, but instead commonly found in trees and other sources away from the ground.

  • Scientific Name: Cinnyricinclus leucogaster
  • Average Length: 20 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 3 years
  • Habitat: Woodlands and savannah forest edges of mainland sub-Saharan Africa

The violet-backed starling (Cinnyricinclus leucogaster), also known as the plum-coloured starling or amethyst starling, is a relatively small species of starling in the family Sturnidae. C. leucogaster is a sexually dimorphic species in which the males possess an iridescent violet back whilst the females are brown.
